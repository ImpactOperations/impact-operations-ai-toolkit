# About Me Builder

Stop getting generic answers from AI. This prompt runs a short interview and builds a personalised file that teaches your AI tool who you are — so every conversation starts with context, not a blank slate.

---

## What this does

Most AI tools give generic answers because they know nothing about you. This fixes that by walking you through a friendly interview and producing a personalised file you can save directly into ChatGPT, Claude, or Gemini.

---

## When to use it

- Use this when you keep having to re-explain your role and organisation every time you open a new AI chat.
- Use this when AI answers feel corporate, hedging, or completely off for your sector.
- Use this when you want AI to write in your voice, not like a press release.
- Use this when a colleague is new to AI and needs a low-friction way to get started.
- Use this when your current AI setup feels like it's talking to a generic person, not you.

---

## How to use it

1. Open `prompt.md` in this folder and copy everything inside the code block.
2. Open a new chat in ChatGPT, Claude, or Gemini.
3. Paste the prompt and send it.
4. Answer the questions as honestly as you can. Voice mode works great here — talk like you're chatting with a friend, not writing a job application.
5. At the end, the AI will produce your personalised file and tell you exactly where to save it.

---

## What you'll get back

A short personalised file written in your voice, covering who you are, how you like to work, what you're focused on right now, and what you do and don't want from AI. For ChatGPT users, it comes in two ready-to-paste sections that fit directly into Custom Instructions. For Claude and Gemini users, it's one clean file to paste into your settings. Once saved, every new chat will start with the AI knowing who you are.

---

## Good to know

- The more honest and specific you are during the interview, the more useful the file will be. Vague answers produce vague results.
- This only applies to new chats. After you save your file, start a fresh conversation to see the difference.
- Update it every few months. Your work changes, and the file should keep up.
